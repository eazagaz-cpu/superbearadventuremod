## Introduction to The Hive

The Hive is one of the most visually stunning and mechanically unique worlds in Super Bear Adventure. Set inside a massive beehive structure, this level introduces honey-based gameplay mechanics that set it apart from every other world in the game. From sticky honey walls you can climb to honey slides that launch you across vast gaps, The Hive demands a different approach than worlds like [Turtletown](/blog/turtletown-walkthrough/) or [Snow Valley](/blog/snow-valley-secrets/).

If you are brand new to Super Bear Adventure, we recommend reading our [beginner guide](/blog/how-to-play-beginner-guide/) first to understand the core controls and movement mechanics before tackling The Hive. This world is generally considered mid-to-late game difficulty, so having a solid grasp of the fundamentals will make your experience much smoother.

In this comprehensive walkthrough, we will cover every area of The Hive, explain all honey mechanics in detail, reveal every secret and collectible location, and provide strategies for the world's challenging boss encounter.

## How to Unlock The Hive

![The Hive entrance gate](/images/worlds/detail/hive-entrance.svg)

The Hive becomes accessible after you have completed at least three main missions in the overworld. You can find the entrance on the eastern side of the main hub area, marked by a large honeycomb archway with buzzing bee NPCs nearby. Walk up to the entrance and interact with the gate to enter.

Before heading in, make sure you have collected enough coins to purchase any helpful items from the shop. ## Understanding Honey Mechanics

The Hive introduces three distinct honey-based mechanics that you need to master to progress through the world. These mechanics are not found in any other world, making The Hive a truly unique experience.

### Sticky Honey Walls

![Wax wall climbing](/images/worlds/detail/hive-wax-walls.svg)

Throughout The Hive, you will encounter walls coated in a golden, glistening honey substance. When your bear touches these walls, it will stick to them, allowing you to climb vertically and even horizontally across ceilings. The controls while stuck to honey walls are straightforward: use the directional pad or joystick to move in any direction along the surface. Press the jump button to detach from the wall and launch yourself in the direction you are facing.

The key to mastering sticky honey walls is understanding momentum. When you detach from a honey wall, your bear launches with a burst of speed in the direction you were moving. This means you can chain wall jumps by moving upward on one honey wall, detaching, and landing on another honey wall across a gap. Several puzzle sections in The Hive require you to chain three or four wall jumps in sequence to reach high platforms.

### Honey Slides

Honey slides are long, curved channels filled with flowing honey that act as high-speed transport tubes. When you step onto a honey slide, your bear is pulled along the current automatically. You cannot stop once you are on a slide, but you can steer left and right to collect coins and avoid obstacles along the path.

Some honey slides have branching paths. At a fork, you need to lean hard in one direction to take the alternate route. The default path usually leads to the next area of the level, while the branching path often leads to a secret area with bonus collectibles or a hidden sticker. Pay close attention to visual cues at forks -- a faint golden glow on one side often indicates the path to a secret.

### Honey Pools

![Honey pools navigation](/images/worlds/detail/hive-honey-pools.svg)

Honey pools are large bodies of honey that act differently from water in other worlds. Instead of swimming, your bear wades slowly through honey pools. Movement speed is reduced by about 60 percent while in honey, and jumping height is cut in half. This makes honey pools dangerous in areas with enemies, as you cannot dodge or escape quickly.

However, honey pools have a hidden benefit: while standing in honey, your bear gradually regenerates health at a rate of about one heart every five seconds. This can be extremely useful during tough sections where you have taken damage. Just find a safe honey pool without enemies and stand still for a moment to heal up before continuing.

## The Hive Walkthrough: Area by Area

### Area 1: The Entrance Hall

When you first enter The Hive, you find yourself in a large hexagonal chamber with golden walls. The Entrance Hall serves as a tutorial area for honey mechanics. On the left side, you will see your first sticky honey wall with a trail of coins leading upward. Climb this wall to reach the upper platform and collect the first batch of coins.

On the right side, there is a short honey slide that takes you down to a lower section. This slide teaches you the basic steering mechanic. At the bottom, you will find three bee enemies. These are standard worker bees that attack by charging at you. A simple punch combo (three hits) defeats them.

After clearing the enemies, look for a small hexagonal door on the far wall. This is the exit to Area 2. Before leaving, check behind the large honeycomb pillar in the center of the room for a hidden coin cache containing 25 coins.

### Area 2: The Honeycomb Corridors

The Honeycomb Corridors are a maze-like section with multiple paths. The main path is straightforward, but exploring side passages reveals secrets and collectibles. The corridors are populated with worker bees and the more dangerous guard bees, which have a shield and require you to attack from behind.

To navigate efficiently, follow the trail of honey drips on the ceiling. They always point toward the main path forward. When you see a corridor without honey drips, that is a side path worth exploring.

The first major secret in this area is hidden behind a breakable honeycomb wall. Look for a section of wall that has a slightly different shade of gold compared to the surrounding walls. Punch it three times to break through and reveal a small room containing a sticker and a large coin pile. For the complete list of sticker locations, see our [secret stickers guide](/blog/all-secret-stickers-locations/).

### Area 3: The Royal Jelly Chamber

This is the largest area in The Hive and serves as the central hub. The Royal Jelly Chamber is a massive vertical room with platforms spiraling upward around the walls. In the center, a column of golden royal jelly flows from the ceiling to the floor.

The goal here is to reach the top of the chamber. You will need to combine all three honey mechanics to ascend. Start by climbing the sticky honey wall on the left side to reach the first platform. From there, take the honey slide to the opposite wall, where another sticky honey wall awaits. Continue alternating between slides and wall climbs as you spiral upward.

At approximately the halfway point, you will encounter a honey pool platform. This is a good place to heal if you have taken damage from the bee enemies that patrol the platforms. Watch out for the flying guard bees in this section, as they can knock you off platforms and force you to restart the climb.

Near the top of the chamber, there is a tricky section where you must detach from a honey wall and land on a moving platform. The platform follows a circular path and is only in range for about two seconds each cycle. Time your jump carefully, and remember that detaching from a honey wall gives you extra momentum.

### Area 4: The Queen's Antechamber

After reaching the top of the Royal Jelly Chamber, you enter the Queen's Antechamber. This area serves as the pre-boss zone and contains the last collectibles before the boss fight. There are no enemies here, giving you a chance to prepare.

Explore the antechamber thoroughly before proceeding. There are three hidden alcoves in the walls, each containing a small coin cache. One alcove also contains the second-to-last sticker in The Hive. To find the alcoves, look for small cracks in the hexagonal wall pattern and punch them to open passages.

At the far end of the antechamber, you will find a health refill station and a save point. Use both before entering the boss arena through the large ornate door.

### Area 5: The Queen Bee Boss Arena

![Queen Bee boss battle](/images/worlds/detail/hive-boss.svg)

The Queen Bee is the boss of The Hive world. For detailed strategies on this and every other boss in the game, check out our comprehensive [boss fight strategy guide](/blog/boss-fight-strategy/). Here, we will cover the essentials specific to this encounter.

The Queen Bee fight takes place in a circular arena with honey walls around the perimeter and a honey pool in the center. The fight has three phases, each introducing new attack patterns.

In phase one, the Queen Bee flies around the arena and periodically dive-bombs your position. Dodge to the side when you see her wind up (she pauses in the air for about one second before diving). After a dive, she is stunned for about three seconds, giving you a window to attack. Three hits during each stun window advances her to the next phase.

In phase two, the Queen Bee summons swarms of worker bees while continuing her dive attacks. The worker bees come in waves of three. Focus on dodging the Queen's dives and deal with the workers between dive attacks. The Queen still gets stunned after dives, but the stun window is shorter at about two seconds.

In phase three, the Queen Bee adds a honey spray attack. She hovers in the center of the arena and sprays honey in a rotating pattern. The honey creates temporary sticky patches on the ground that slow your movement. Use the honey walls around the arena to climb above the spray and then drop down on the Queen when she finishes spraying. This is your attack window in phase three, and three hits during this window will defeat her.

## All Secrets in The Hive

The Hive contains a total of 12 secrets spread across its five areas. Finding all of them contributes to your overall completion percentage and unlocks a special cosmetic reward. For a broader look at secrets across all worlds, visit our [secret areas and easter eggs guide](/blog/secret-areas-easter-eggs/).

### Secret 1: Hidden Coin Room (Entrance Hall)

Behind the central pillar in the Entrance Hall, punch the back wall to reveal a hidden room with 50 coins.

### Secret 2: Breakable Wall Sticker (Honeycomb Corridors)

In the Honeycomb Corridors, find the differently-shaded wall section and break through to find a sticker and coins.

### Secret 3: Ceiling Passage (Honeycomb Corridors)

In the third corridor from the entrance, there is a sticky honey patch on the ceiling. Climb across it to reach a hidden upper passage with a large gem worth 100 coins.

### Secret 4: Reverse Honey Slide (Honeycomb Corridors)

One of the honey slides in the corridors can be entered from the exit end by performing a well-timed jump. Going backward through the slide reveals a hidden alcove midway through with a rare collectible.

### Secret 5: False Floor (Royal Jelly Chamber)

On the third platform while spiraling upward, one of the hexagonal floor tiles is slightly raised. Step on it to trigger a trapdoor that drops you into a secret room below with a sticker and 75 coins. Use the honey wall inside the room to climb back up.

### Secret 6: Behind the Royal Jelly Column

When you reach the midpoint of the Royal Jelly Chamber, jump into the royal jelly column itself. Instead of taking damage, you are transported to a hidden room behind the column containing a rare hat unlock. Check our [skins and hats guide](/blog/unlock-all-skins-hats/) for the full list of unlockable cosmetics.

### Secret 7: Moving Platform Secret (Royal Jelly Chamber)

Instead of jumping onto the moving platform near the top of the chamber, wait for it to pass and then jump to the small ledge behind where the platform docks. A hidden passage leads to a bonus area with 150 coins.

### Secret 8: Double-Back Corridor (Royal Jelly Chamber)

After reaching the top of the chamber, instead of going forward into the antechamber, look behind you for a small honey wall leading to a separate upper area with a sticker.

### Secret 9: Alcove Sticker (Queen's Antechamber)

One of the three wall alcoves in the antechamber contains a sticker. Check the right wall first.

### Secret 10: Floor Pattern Puzzle (Queen's Antechamber)

Step on the hexagonal floor tiles in a specific pattern (center, top-right, bottom-left, top-left, bottom-right, center) to open a hidden compartment in the floor containing a golden honeycomb collectible worth 200 coins.

### Secret 11: Boss Arena Honey Pool (Boss Arena)

During the boss fight, if you stand in the center honey pool for ten consecutive seconds without being hit, a secret platform rises from the pool with a sticker on it.

### Secret 12: Post-Boss Secret Room

After defeating the Queen Bee, instead of going through the victory door immediately, return to the entrance of the arena. A previously blocked passage is now open, leading to a room with the final sticker and a large coin reward.

## Tips for Completing The Hive

Mastering The Hive comes down to comfort with the honey mechanics. Here are some additional tips to help you through.

### Movement Tips

Always keep moving on honey walls. Staying still causes your bear to slowly slide downward due to gravity. Maintain upward momentum by constantly pressing in the direction you want to go.

On honey slides, position yourself toward the outer edge of curves to maintain maximum speed. The inner edge slows you down slightly, which can cause you to miss jump opportunities at the end of slides.

### Combat Tips

Guard bees are best handled by using the honey walls to get above them and then dropping down with a ground pound attack. This bypasses their front shield entirely.

During the Queen Bee fight, stay near the arena walls so you can quickly climb to safety during the honey spray attack in phase three.

### Collection Tips

Do not worry about getting every secret on your first playthrough. You can replay The Hive at any time from the world select menu to pick up anything you missed. Focus on completing the main path first and then return for collectibles.
