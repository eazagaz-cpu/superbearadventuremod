## Introduction to Giant House

Giant House is one of the most creative and visually striking worlds in Super Bear Adventure. The entire concept revolves around a single brilliant idea: you are a tiny bear exploring an ordinary house where everything is scaled to enormous proportions. Kitchen tables tower above you like skyscrapers. Pencils on desks become balance beams. A simple staircase becomes a mountainous climb. Every familiar household object is transformed into a platforming challenge or environmental puzzle.

This world tests your platforming skills more than almost any other area in the game. The oversized environment means that falls are more punishing, jumps require more precision, and navigation demands careful planning. But the rewards for thorough exploration are substantial, with some of the rarest stickers and most generous coin deposits hidden in the most unexpected places.

This guide provides a complete room-by-room walkthrough of Giant House, covering every mission objective, secret area, puzzle solution, collectible, and boss strategy. Whether you are visiting Giant House for the first time or returning to find the last few secrets you missed, this walkthrough has everything you need. If you are still getting comfortable with the game's controls and mechanics, our [beginner guide](/blog/how-to-play-beginner-guide/) is a great place to start before taking on this challenging world.

## How to Access Giant House

Giant House becomes available after you have progressed far enough in the main game. Here is exactly what you need to do to unlock it:

1. **Complete prior world requirements.** Giant House typically requires completion of at least two other worlds' main mission lines. If you have finished Turtletown and another world like Beemothep Desert, you should be eligible. Our [Turtletown walkthrough](/blog/turtletown-walkthrough/) and [Beemothep Desert puzzle guide](/blog/beemothep-desert-puzzle/) can help you finish those worlds.
2. **Gather sufficient stickers.** You will need approximately 25 to 30 stickers to open the Giant House portal. Check our [all secret stickers locations](/blog/all-secret-stickers-locations/) guide if you need to boost your sticker count.
3. **Locate the portal in the hub world.** The Giant House portal is designed to look like a tiny mouse hole at the base of a large wall in the hub area. It is easy to walk right past because of its small size. Look along the northern wall of the hub near the fountain.
4. **Enter the mouse hole.** Walk into the mouse hole and your bear will shrink down, transitioning you into the Giant House world. You spawn inside a crack in the baseboard of the house's main hallway.

## Understanding the Giant House Mechanics

Giant House introduces several unique gameplay mechanics that set it apart from every other world in Super Bear Adventure.

### Scale and Perspective

Everything in the house is roughly 20 times your size. This means that objects you would normally walk past in real life become major obstacles or platforms. A coffee mug is the size of a small building. A book lying on a table forms a ramp. A pencil becomes a tightrope. Understanding this scale is critical for navigating the environment because the game uses realistic proportions. If something looks climbable in real life, it probably is in the game.

### Vertical Navigation

Giant House is the most vertical world in the game. You will spend a significant amount of time climbing upward, whether it is scaling table legs, bouncing off cushions, or riding moving objects like ceiling fans or toy trains. Falls from great heights deal substantial damage, so move carefully and look for checkpoints frequently.

### Surface Physics

Different surfaces in the house have different physics properties:

- **Wood surfaces** (tables, shelves, floors): Normal traction and movement speed.
- **Tile surfaces** (kitchen, bathroom): Slightly slippery, especially when wet.
- **Fabric surfaces** (cushions, carpets, towels): Bouncy. Jumping on fabric gives you extra height.
- **Metal surfaces** (pots, pans, appliances): Normal traction but often hot in the kitchen, causing damage on contact with surfaces near the stove.
- **Glass surfaces** (windows, mirrors, jars): Very slippery. Minimal traction and easy to slide off.

### Household Hazards

The house is filled with environmental hazards that reflect the dangers a tiny creature would face:

- **Running water** from faucets acts as a powerful current that pushes you.
- **Hot surfaces** near the stove and oven deal damage on contact.
- **Electrical outlets** spark periodically and create small shock zones.
- **Mousetraps** are instant-kill hazards scattered in corners and along baseboards.
- **The house cat** patrols certain rooms and acts as a roaming enemy.

## Room-by-Room Walkthrough

Giant House contains seven main rooms plus several connecting areas. Each room has its own set of missions, secrets, and challenges. Below is a complete walkthrough of every room in the recommended exploration order.

### The Main Hallway (Starting Area)

You enter Giant House through a crack in the baseboard of the main hallway. This area serves as the hub for the world, with doorways leading to each room.

**Missions in this area:**

- Collect the three lost buttons scattered along the hallway floor.
- Reach the top of the coat rack near the front door.
- Find the hidden key under the doormat (used to access the basement later).

**Secrets:**

- A secret sticker is hidden inside one of the shoes on the shoe rack. Jump inside the left boot and drop down to find it.
- Coins are lined along the top of the baseboard molding. Wall jump up to reach them.
- A small hole behind the umbrella stand leads to a shortcut passage between the hallway and the kitchen.

**Tips:** The hallway is relatively safe and serves as a good place to practice the unique movement mechanics before entering more dangerous rooms. Take time to get comfortable with the scale and jumping distances here.

### The Kitchen

![Giant kitchen navigation](/images/worlds/detail/giant-kitchen.svg)

The kitchen is the largest and most complex room in Giant House. It is also one of the most dangerous due to hot surfaces and water hazards.

**Missions in this area:**

- Cross the kitchen counter from the toaster to the sink without touching the floor.
- Retrieve the golden spoon from inside the utensil drawer.
- Shut off the leaking faucet by turning the oversized handle.
- Defeat the cockroach miniboss that lives under the refrigerator.

**Navigation Guide:**

1. Enter the kitchen through the doorway from the hallway. You start at floor level.
2. Climb the first chair by jumping up the cross-support bars on the chair leg. This takes about four precise jumps.
3. From the chair seat, jump to the kitchen table. Use the napkin holder as a stepping stone.
4. From the table, you can reach the counter by jumping to the hanging dish towel and climbing up.
5. On the counter, navigate past the toaster (it periodically pops up toast that can launch you), the knife block (avoid the sharp edges), and the fruit bowl (you can climb the fruit for height).
6. Reach the sink area. The dripping faucet creates water puddles that push you. Time your movements between drips.
7. The utensil drawer can be opened by pushing a button on the counter near the drawer. Slide down into the drawer to collect the golden spoon.

**Secrets:**

- Inside the sugar jar on the counter (jump in through the open lid) is a secret sticker.
- The microwave can be entered if you hit the button on the front. Inside are coins and a collectible item.
- Under the refrigerator is a hidden area accessible from a gap at the back. This is also where the cockroach miniboss spawns.

**Tips:** The stove burners cycle on and off on a timer. Watch the pattern for about ten seconds before trying to cross the stovetop area. The burners stay off for roughly five seconds at a time, which is enough to dash across if you move quickly.

### The Living Room

The living room is the most platforming-intensive room, featuring tall bookshelves, a massive sofa, and a TV entertainment center that serves as the room's main vertical challenge.

**Missions in this area:**

- Climb to the top of the bookshelf and collect the photo frame.
- Ride the ceiling fan for a full rotation without falling.
- Find all five remote control batteries scattered around the room.
- Activate the TV by pressing the power button on the entertainment center.

**Navigation Guide:**

1. Enter from the hallway. The living room floor is covered in carpet, giving you bouncy movement.
2. The sofa is the central landmark. Jump onto the cushions for extra height. The bounce from the cushions can launch you to the coffee table or to the sofa arm.
3. From the sofa arm, jump to the side table and then to the lamp. The lampshade acts as a slide that deposits you on the bookshelf.
4. The bookshelf has multiple levels you can climb by jumping between books that stick out at different depths.
5. At the top of the bookshelf, you can reach the ceiling fan by jumping to the pull chain and swinging to the fan blade.
6. The entertainment center against the far wall has shelves you can climb. The TV power button is on the bottom edge of the TV, accessible from the entertainment center's middle shelf.

**Secrets:**

- Behind the sofa cushions. Push the middle cushion by attacking it three times, and it slides to reveal a gap. Drop in for a secret sticker and a coin cache.
- Inside the potted plant next to the bookshelf. Climb the plant and drop into the pot's soil for a hidden collectible.
- On top of the ceiling fan. After riding a full rotation, jump to the central hub of the fan where a treasure chest sits.

**Tips:** The ceiling fan is one of the trickiest platforming challenges in the entire game. The blades move at a moderate speed, and you need to maintain your balance while they rotate. Stay near the wide end of the blade for the most stable footing. If you fall, you land on the sofa cushions below and can try again without taking fall damage.

### The Bedroom

![Giant bedroom exploration](/images/worlds/detail/giant-bedroom.svg)

The bedroom features a massive bed as its centerpiece, along with a desk, wardrobe, and toy chest that all serve as platforming structures.

**Missions in this area:**

- Collect the lost teddy bear's button eye from the desk.
- Open the music box on the nightstand.
- Explore under the bed and clear out the dust bunny enemies.
- Reach the top of the wardrobe.

**Navigation Guide:**

1. Enter from the hallway. The bedroom has hardwood floors, so traction is normal.
2. The bed is climbable via the dangling bedsheet on the side. Jump to the sheet and climb up like a rope.
3. From the bed, you can jump to the nightstand and then to the desk.
4. The desk has stacked books, a lamp, and scattered stationery that form a platforming path to the upper desk shelves.
5. The wardrobe is the tallest structure in the room. Reach it by jumping from the desk to a hanging picture frame, then swinging to the wardrobe's decorative molding and climbing up.

**Secrets:**

- Under the bed is an entire hidden area with dust bunny enemies and a secret sticker in the far corner behind a lost sock.
- The toy chest can be opened by ground-pounding its latch. Inside are coins and a unique toy soldier cosmetic item.
- The music box, when activated, plays a melody that opens a secret panel on the wall behind the nightstand. Inside the panel is a treasure chest.

**Tips:** The dust bunnies under the bed are weak individually but attack in groups. Use area attacks or spin moves to handle multiple enemies at once. The area under the bed is dark, so watch for their glowing eyes to track their positions.

### The Bathroom

The bathroom is the most hazard-dense room due to water everywhere. It features a bathtub, toilet, and sink that each present unique challenges.

**Missions in this area:**

- Navigate across the flooded floor to reach the medicine cabinet.
- Collect the rubber duck from the bathtub.
- Drain the overflowing sink by removing the stopper.
- Find the hidden pipe passage to the basement.

**Navigation Guide:**

1. The bathroom floor is partially flooded with shallow water. Movement through water is slow, so use the bath mat and scattered items as dry stepping stones.
2. The toilet is climbable via the towel hanging from a rack nearby. Use it as a swing to reach the toilet seat, then jump to the tank top.
3. From the toilet tank, jump to the sink counter. The soap dish and toothbrush holder serve as platforms.
4. The medicine cabinet above the sink can be reached by climbing the mirror frame. Inside the cabinet, shelves hold collectibles and mission items.
5. The bathtub is accessible via the bath mat. The rubber duck floats in the water and acts as a moving platform.

**Secrets:**

- Inside the drain of the bathtub (when you manage to pull the plug) is a pipe passage leading to a hidden underground room with a treasure chest.
- Behind the toilet is a secret sticker wedged between the toilet and the wall.
- The medicine cabinet's top shelf has a secret compartment that opens when you push a specific bottle to the left.

**Tips:** The flooded floor creates a current that pushes you toward the drain. Stay on the bath mat and dry objects when possible. If you do get swept into the current, jump repeatedly to slow your drift and work your way to the nearest solid ground.

### The Attic

![Giant attic secrets](/images/worlds/detail/giant-attic.svg)

The attic is an optional area accessed by climbing through a hatch in the bedroom ceiling. It is one of the most rewarding areas in Giant House.

**Missions in this area:**

- Find the three pieces of the torn photograph.
- Activate the old radio.
- Clear out the spider web barriers blocking the back of the attic.

**Navigation Guide:**

1. Access the attic from the top of the bedroom wardrobe. A ceiling hatch is within jumping distance from the wardrobe's top.
2. The attic is filled with cardboard boxes, old furniture, and holiday decorations. These form natural platforms and pathways.
3. Spider webs block several areas. Attack them to clear them, but be prepared for spider enemies to drop from above.
4. The old radio is on a high shelf at the back of the attic. Reach it by climbing stacked boxes.

**Secrets:**

- A treasure chest sits inside a hollowed-out globe on the shelf near the window.
- A secret sticker is taped to the underside of a cardboard box flap. You need to jump under the flap and look up to spot it.
- Christmas ornaments hanging from the rafters contain coins when broken.

**Tips:** The attic spiders are the strongest basic enemies in Giant House. They can web-shoot to slow you down and they drop from the ceiling without warning. Listen for the subtle clicking sound that precedes their ambush attacks.

### The Basement

The basement is the final area of Giant House and the location of the world boss. It is dark, damp, and filled with the toughest enemies in the world.

**Missions in this area:**

- Restore power to the basement by flipping the breaker switch.
- Navigate through the storage shelves to find the boss arena.
- Defeat the Giant House boss.

**Navigation Guide:**

1. Access the basement either through the hidden pipe passage in the bathroom or by using the key found under the hallway doormat to unlock the basement door.
2. The basement is initially dark. Navigate carefully using the faint light from small windows until you reach the breaker panel.
3. After restoring power, the basement lights up, revealing storage shelves, a workbench, and a furnace. The boss arena is in the cleared area behind the furnace.

**Secrets:**

- The workbench has a vice grip holding a treasure chest. Interact with the vice handle to release it.
- Behind the water heater is a secret sticker and a pile of coins.
- A loose brick in the basement wall near the stairs can be pushed to reveal a small nook with a rare collectible.

## The Giant House Boss Fight

![House Cat boss battle](/images/worlds/detail/giant-boss.svg)

The Giant House boss is the House Cat, a massive feline that has been patrolling certain rooms throughout your exploration. In the basement, you confront it in a proper boss arena.

### Phase 1: Pounce and Swipe

The cat starts by pouncing at your location and swiping with its paws. Both attacks are telegraphed by the cat crouching down before pouncing or raising a paw before swiping.

- **Dodge the pounce** by moving to the side when you see the crouch animation. The pounce has a fixed trajectory and the cat cannot change direction mid-leap.
- **After each pounce**, the cat is momentarily stunned when it lands. Run in and attack its tail or hind legs during this window.
- **Dodge swipes** by staying outside the arc of the paw. The swipe has a wide range but only covers about 120 degrees in front of the cat.

### Phase 2: Speed Chase

After losing about one-third of its health, the cat speeds up and begins chasing you around the arena. It knocks over objects as it runs, creating new obstacles and occasionally opening new paths.

- **Use the knocked-over objects** as barriers. The cat will pause briefly when it collides with a large object, giving you an attack window.
- **Climb onto shelves** when the cat is in full pursuit. It cannot follow you up high and will pace below, occasionally swiping at the shelf. Jump down and strike when it turns away.

### Phase 3: Cornered Cat

In the final phase, the cat retreats to a corner and begins launching ranged attacks by knocking small objects at you with its paws. It also summons mouse enemies as distractions.

- **Dodge the thrown objects** by watching what the cat picks up. Larger objects move slower and are easier to avoid.
- **Ignore the mice** unless they are directly threatening you. Focus all your attacks on the cat during the brief pauses between its throws.
- **The final blow** can be struck when the cat rears up on its hind legs after throwing three objects in rapid succession. This leaves its belly exposed for about three seconds.

### Boss Tips

- Bring health items if you have them. This fight is one of the longer boss encounters in the game.
- The bouncy carpet in the arena can be used to gain extra jump height, which is useful for reaching the cat's head for bonus damage.
- For general combat strategies that apply across all boss fights, check our [boss fight strategy](/blog/boss-fight-strategy/) guide.

## Movement Tips for the Giant Environment

Navigating a world where everything is oversized requires adjustments to your usual approach. Here are specific movement strategies for Giant House:

- **Master wall jumping.** Many surfaces in the house are flat walls that support wall jumps. Use wall jumps to climb chair legs, table legs, and doorframes.
- **Use fabric for height.** Whenever you see cushions, towels, or carpet, jump on them for extra bounce height. Chaining bounces on multiple fabric surfaces can launch you to surprising heights.
- **Look for everyday objects as paths.** Pencils, rulers, spoons, and similar objects lying across gaps serve as bridges. If something is long and thin, you can probably walk across it.
- **Be cautious near edges.** The oversized scale means falls deal more damage than in other worlds. Edges of tables and shelves are particularly dangerous because the landing surface is often far below.
- **Use the slide move on slopes.** Tilted books, ramps made from rulers, and angled surfaces all support the slide move, which gives you speed and style points.

## Coin Farming Locations in Giant House

Giant House offers several efficient coin farming routes:

1. **Kitchen counter circuit:** Run from the toaster to the sink and back. Enemies and coin pickups along this path yield about 100 coins per run in approximately one minute.
2. **Living room cushion bounce:** Repeatedly bouncing on the sofa cushions spawns coin rings in the air above the cushions. A minute of bouncing can generate roughly 80 coins.
3. **Under the bed clear:** The dust bunnies under the bed respawn when you leave and re-enter the bedroom. Each clear of the dust bunnies drops around 120 coins.
4. **Attic spider runs:** The attic spiders drop more coins than any other basic enemy in Giant House. A full attic clear is worth approximately 200 coins.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does it take to complete Giant House?

A focused playthrough covering all main missions takes approximately 45 minutes to one hour. Finding every secret and collectible can extend that to two hours or more.

### What is the hardest part of Giant House?

Most players find the ceiling fan challenge in the living room to be the most difficult platforming section. The kitchen stovetop crossing and the attic spider encounters are also commonly cited as challenging areas.

### Can I leave Giant House and come back later?

Yes. All progress within Giant House is saved. You can return to the hub world at any time through the mouse hole entrance and come back to pick up where you left off.

### Do I need to complete Giant House to access later worlds?

Giant House completion contributes to your overall sticker count and mission progress, which may be required for accessing later worlds. However, the specific requirements vary. Check our [all missions guide](/blog/all-missions-guide/) for the full progression path.

### The game runs slowly in Giant House. How can I fix it?

Giant House has more complex geometry than most worlds due to the detailed room environments. If you experience lag, our [lag fix and optimization](/blog/lag-fix-optimization/) guide has specific settings recommendations. Lowering shadow quality and reducing particle effects will have the biggest impact in this world.

### I cannot find the basement key. Where is it?

The basement key is under the doormat at the front door in the main hallway. You need to ground-pound the doormat to reveal it. It is one of the first things available in the world, but many players overlook it.

### Is there a way to fast travel between rooms?

There is no formal fast travel system within Giant House. However, the shortcut passage behind the umbrella stand in the hallway connects directly to the kitchen, and the bathroom pipe passage connects to the basement. These shortcuts save significant travel time once discovered.

## Final Thoughts on Giant House

Giant House is a masterclass in creative world design. The room-by-room structure also makes it one of the most approachable worlds for completionists since you can methodically clear one area before moving to the next.
