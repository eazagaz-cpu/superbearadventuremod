The Kingdom of Earthkwak is the setting of Super Bear Adventure — a fantasy world consisting of 6 distinct regions, each with its own ecosystem, culture, and threat. This guide covers the complete lore, the story of each region, and how the world hangs together as a coherent setting.

## The Kingdom Overview

The Kingdom of Earthkwak is named after its founding civilization, which built the structures and culture that underpin each region. The kingdom is ancient — the ruins in Beemothep Desert, the underground hive civilization, and the deep mines of Mole Mines all predate the current political situation.

Baaren is not a prince or a warrior — he is a bear from Turtletown who discovers the kingdom is under threat from multiple directions simultaneously. The game implies these threats are connected at a higher level, though the exact connection is left to player interpretation.

## The Six Regions

### Region 1: Turtletown

**Ecosystem:** Coastal village, tropical beach, shallow ocean  
**Inhabitants:** Turtles, bears (Baaren's home community)  
**Ruler:** Town Elder (unnamed, friendly NPC)  
**Villain:** Turtle King (corrupt turtle who has turned aggressive)

**Lore:** Turtletown is the oldest settled community in the kingdom. Turtles and bears have coexisted peacefully here for generations. When the Turtle King — historically the protector of the ocean shoreline — turns aggressive and begins hoarding resources, Baaren must confront his former protector to restore peace.

**Narrative role:** The tutorial world. Turtletown represents the stable, peaceful life that Baaren is defending across the entire game.

**Connection to other worlds:** After defeating the Turtle King, dialogue reveals that someone (unknown) has been influencing the villain across all regions — Baaren decides to investigate.

### Region 2: Snow Valley

**Ecosystem:** Alpine mountains, frozen lakes, ice caves, snow-covered forests  
**Inhabitants:** Snow bears, arctic creatures, winter birds  
**Ruler:** Mountain Elder (wise snowy owl NPC)  
**Villain:** Frost Giant (ancient ice creature awakened from glacial sleep)

**Lore:** Snow Valley is the most geographically remote region. The Frost Giant has slept beneath the glacier for centuries. An earthquake (implied to be caused by the same overarching villain) awakened him. The Frost Giant is not malicious — he is confused and frightened — but his giant movements are destroying Snow Valley.

**Narrative role:** Introduces the idea that some villains are not evil, just misguided. Baaren's approach in Snow Valley involves calming the Frost Giant rather than simply defeating him — though the boss fight still happens.

**Connection to other worlds:** The Mountain Elder tells Baaren about ancient ruins in the Desert where records of the kingdom's founding might reveal why these crises are happening simultaneously.

For the full walkthrough, see [Snow Valley guide](/blog/snow-valley-secrets/).

### Region 3: Beemothep Desert

**Ecosystem:** Arid desert, ancient ruins, pyramids, underground tombs  
**Inhabitants:** Desert foxes, scarab beetles, ancient guardian statues  
**Ruler:** None — the previous ruler was the Sand Pharaoh before his corruption  
**Villain:** Sand Pharaoh (ancient ruler awakened from death by the mystery force)

**Lore:** Beemothep Desert contains the oldest structures in the kingdom — predating Turtletown civilization by thousands of years. The Sand Pharaoh ruled a sophisticated civilization that collapsed due to unknown reasons. He has been awakened from death and is trying to reestablish his rule using the desert's ancient magic.

**Narrative role:** The deepest lore content in the game. Hieroglyphic inscriptions in the pyramid hint at the kingdom's founding story. A stone tablet here is the first concrete clue about the overarching villain.

**Connection to other worlds:** The stone tablet mentions a "Hive Council" that supposedly maintains balance across the kingdom — pointing toward The Hive as the key location.

For the full walkthrough, see [Desert guide](/blog/beemothep-desert-puzzle/).

### Region 4: Giant House

**Ecosystem:** An impossibly large house, where furniture is the landscape  
**Inhabitants:** The Giant (friendly but clumsy), various small creatures  
**Ruler:** The Giant (giant human who owns the house — benign)  
**Villain:** House Cat (the Giant's pet cat who has grown oversized and aggressive)

**Lore:** The Giant House is the most surreal region. A genuinely enormous human lives here, completely unaware that tiny bears and creatures inhabit the spaces between his furniture. His cat has grown unusually large and aggressive, terrorizing the small inhabitants.

**Narrative role:** The lightest-toned world, providing contrast to the heavier lore of the Desert and The Hive. The Giant himself is friendly and a source of comic relief.

**Connection to other worlds:** A diary entry found in the attic mentions the Giant receiving an unusual "gift" from a trader — the same trader suspected of influencing the other villains.

For the full walkthrough, see [Giant House guide](/blog/giant-house-walkthrough/).

### Region 5: The Hive

**Ecosystem:** Underground bee civilization, honey structures, vertical hive chambers  
**Inhabitants:** Worker bees, guard bees, bee artisans, the Queen  
**Ruler:** Queen Bee (corrupted)  
**Villain:** Queen Bee (formerly benevolent, now corrupted by the mystery influence)

**Lore:** The Hive is the most politically significant region. The Hive Council mentioned in the Desert tablet is real — it is the governing body of the bee civilization underground. The Queen was the most respected ruler in the kingdom. Her corruption destabilizes the kingdom's social fabric more than any other villain.

**Narrative role:** The emotional climax of the storyline. Baaren discovers that defeating the Queen is necessary but genuinely sad — she was the kingdom's most important figure before corruption.

**Connection to other worlds:** After the Queen is defeated, she recovers enough to explain what corrupted her — and reveals the mystery villain's identity and location: the Mole Mines.

For the full walkthrough, see [The Hive guide](/blog/the-hive-walkthrough/).

### Region 6: Mole Mines

**Ecosystem:** Deep underground crystal caverns, mining tunnels, underground lakes  
**Inhabitants:** Moles (miners), cave creatures, crystal golems  
**Ruler:** Mole Council (collective governing body)  
**Villain:** Mole King (betrayed the Mole Council, has been the source of all corruption)

**Lore:** The Mole King is the overarching villain. He discovered ancient technology in the deepest mine shafts — technology left by the original Kingdom of Earthkwak founders. Using this technology, he has been sending corrupting signals to the other regions, awakening and influencing each villain.

**Narrative role:** The final confrontation. Defeating the Mole King concludes the main story.

**Post-credits:** The ending sequence shows each region recovering — Turtle King returns to his protective role, the Frost Giant returns to slumber, the Sand Pharaoh's spirit rests, the Giant's cat shrinks back to normal, the Queen Bee begins healing. The kingdom is restored.

For the full walkthrough, see [Mole Mines guide](/blog/mole-mines-walkthrough/).

## Timeline of Events

1. **Ancient era:** Kingdom of Earthkwak founded. Hive Council established as inter-regional governing body. Ancient technology sealed in Mole Mines.
2. **Historical era:** Sand Pharaoh's civilization rises and falls. Desert region abandoned.
3. **Recent past:** Mole King discovers ancient technology. Begins sending corrupting signals to other regions.
4. **Game events:** Baaren's adventure begins in Turtletown. Six regions visited, six villains defeated, kingdom restored.
