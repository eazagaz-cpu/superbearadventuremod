## Welcome to the Mole Mines

The Mole Mines is one of the most thrilling worlds in Super Bear Adventure, taking players deep underground into a sprawling network of tunnels, caverns, and crystal-filled chambers. Unlike the open-air environments of [Turtletown](/blog/turtletown-walkthrough/) or [Snow Valley](/blog/snow-valley-secrets/), the Mole Mines feel claustrophobic and intense, with tight corridors, darkness mechanics, and high-speed mining cart sections that test your reflexes.

This world introduces two major gameplay systems: mining cart rides where you must dodge obstacles and switch tracks at high speed, and gem puzzles that require you to collect and place colored gems in specific patterns to unlock new areas. Both mechanics create a gameplay loop that is distinct from anything else in Super Bear Adventure.

If you are new to the game, we strongly recommend completing our [beginner guide](/blog/how-to-play-beginner-guide/) first. The Mole Mines is considered an advanced world, and understanding core mechanics like combat, jumping, and item management will help you avoid frustration in the more demanding sections.

## How to Access the Mole Mines

![Mole Mines entrance](/images/worlds/detail/mines-entrance.svg)

The Mole Mines entrance is located on the northern edge of the overworld hub, behind a large boulder. To move the boulder, you need to have completed at least four main missions. Once the boulder is cleared, a mine shaft elevator becomes accessible. Ride it down to enter the Mole Mines.

Before descending, stock up on supplies. The Mole Mines has fewer health pickups than most other worlds, so purchasing extra health items from the shop is wise. If you need coins for supplies, our [coin farming guide](/blog/unlimited-coins-farming/) has efficient strategies for building your funds quickly.

## Mining Cart Mechanics Explained

![Mining cart ride through the tunnels](/images/worlds/detail/mines-cart-ride.svg)

Mining cart sections are the signature feature of the Mole Mines. These are on-rails segments where your bear rides in a mine cart through twisting tunnels at high speed. Understanding how these sections work is essential for survival and for finding secrets.

### Basic Controls

During mining cart sections, your bear automatically moves forward on the track. You have three actions available. First, you can lean left or right to shift the cart between parallel tracks. This is essential for avoiding obstacles like fallen rocks and broken track sections. Second, you can jump by pressing the jump button, which makes the cart briefly leave the track to hop over gaps and low barriers. Third, you can duck by pressing the crouch button, which lowers your bear's profile to pass under low-hanging stalactites and wooden beams.

### Track Switching

At certain points during cart rides, the track splits into two or three parallel paths. A visual indicator appears on screen showing which tracks are available. Leaning left or right switches between tracks. The key detail many players miss is that track switches are not instant. There is about a half-second transition period where your cart moves laterally. During this transition, you are vulnerable to obstacles on both the origin and destination tracks. Plan your switches early when you see an obstacle ahead rather than waiting until the last moment.

### Speed Boosts and Brakes

Some track sections have golden rail segments that boost your speed. These are fun but also make reaction windows shorter for upcoming obstacles. Red rail segments slow you down. While slower sections might feel like a reprieve, they often precede the most obstacle-dense portions of the track, so stay alert.

### Collectibles on Tracks

Coins and gems are scattered along the tracks during cart rides. They usually appear in lines that indicate the safe track to follow. If you see a line of coins on the left track, it is generally safe to stay on that track until the coins end. However, some advanced sections deliberately place coins on dangerous tracks to tempt greedy players. Trust obstacle cues over coin placement when they conflict.

## Gem Puzzle System

![Gem puzzle mechanics in the Mole Mines](/images/worlds/detail/mines-gem-puzzle.svg)

The Mole Mines features a color-coded gem puzzle system that gates progression through the world. There are four gem colors: red, blue, green, and yellow. Each gem type is found in specific areas of the mines, and you need to collect gems and place them in matching pedestals to unlock doors and activate mechanisms.

### Finding Gems

Gems are not just lying on the ground. Most are earned by completing challenges within the Mole Mines. Red gems come from defeating mini-boss mole enemies in combat arenas. Blue gems are rewards for completing mining cart sections without taking damage. Green gems are hidden in secret areas behind breakable walls. Yellow gems are obtained by solving environmental puzzles like pressure plate sequences and lever combinations.

### Gem Pedestals

Throughout the Mole Mines, you will find hexagonal pedestals with colored gem-shaped slots. Walking up to a pedestal with the matching gem in your inventory automatically places the gem. Each pedestal is part of a larger puzzle -- some doors require one gem, while the final door before the boss requires all four colors.

### The Master Gem Gate

The most complex puzzle in the Mole Mines is the Master Gem Gate, located in Area 5. This gate requires you to place gems in a specific sequence. The correct order is revealed by examining the murals on the walls of the surrounding cavern. The murals depict the four gem colors in order from top to bottom: green, red, yellow, blue. Place the gems in this order to open the gate. Placing them in the wrong order resets all four pedestals, forcing you to replace them.

## Area-by-Area Walkthrough

### Area 1: The Upper Tunnels

You begin in a series of dimly lit tunnels close to the surface. The lighting here is slightly better than deeper areas, easing you into the underground atmosphere. The Upper Tunnels are primarily a combat zone with groups of mole enemies.

Mole enemies come in two types. Digger moles pop out of the ground and attack with their claws. They are vulnerable for about two seconds after surfacing. Armored moles wear helmets and require a ground pound attack to stun them before you can deal damage with normal attacks.

The Upper Tunnels contain your first red gem. To earn it, defeat the mini-boss digger mole in the large cavern at the end of the area. This mole is larger than normal digger moles, has three times the health, and can burrow and resurface behind you. Keep moving in circles and attack during its surface windows.

A side tunnel on the left leads to a small crystal cave with a hidden sticker. For all sticker locations across every world, see our [sticker locations guide](/blog/all-secret-stickers-locations/).

### Area 2: The Crystal Caverns

The Crystal Caverns are a breathtaking open area filled with enormous glowing crystals in various colors. The cavern is the vertical hub of the Mole Mines, with paths branching off at different heights. You will return here multiple times as you explore deeper areas.

The main feature of the Crystal Caverns is the crystal light puzzle. Large crystals can be rotated by punching them, and when rotated to the correct angle, they refract light beams to illuminate dark passages. There are three crystals that need to be aligned to open the path to Area 3.

Crystal one should be rotated twice (two punches) to point its beam at the mirror on the west wall. Crystal two needs four punches to direct its beam to the east mirror. Crystal three needs one punch to send its beam to the ceiling reflector, which splits the light and illuminates the entire cavern, revealing the previously hidden entrance to Area 3.

While in the Crystal Caverns, explore the lower level by dropping down from the main platform. A series of small ledges leads to a hidden alcove with a green gem and 100 bonus coins.

### Area 3: The Mine Cart Express

Area 3 is the first major mining cart section. This is a long, continuous cart ride through winding tunnels with increasing difficulty.

The ride begins slowly with simple obstacles: single rocks on the track that require a lane change, and low beams that require ducking. After about 30 seconds, the speed increases and obstacles come in combinations. You might need to switch lanes and immediately duck, or jump a gap and then switch lanes before landing.

The midpoint of the ride features a dramatic section where the track runs along the edge of a massive underground chasm. The track itself is partially broken here, requiring precise jumps over three consecutive gaps. The timing is tight -- jump as soon as the front of the cart reaches the broken edge.

At the end of the cart ride, if you completed the section without taking any damage, you receive a blue gem. If you took damage, you can replay the section by walking back to the cart loading area.

There is a secret during this cart ride. About halfway through, during the chasm section, look for a side track branching to the right just before the second gap. Switch to the right track instead of jumping the gap. This side track leads to a hidden room with a sticker and a shortcut back to the Crystal Caverns.

### Area 4: The Deep Tunnels

The Deep Tunnels are the darkest area of the Mole Mines. Your visibility is limited to a small radius around your bear, lit by a helmet lamp that activates automatically. The limited visibility makes enemy encounters more tense, as mole enemies can ambush you from the darkness.

Navigation in the Deep Tunnels relies on sound cues and environmental hints. Dripping water sounds indicate a turn to the right. Rumbling sounds mean an armored mole is nearby. A faint musical chime means you are near a secret or collectible.

The yellow gem is obtained in the Deep Tunnels by solving a lever puzzle. There are five levers spread across the area, each labeled with a symbol. The correct order to pull them is: pickaxe, gem, cart, mole, bear. This order is hinted at by a sign near the entrance to the Deep Tunnels, though the sign is partially obscured and easy to miss.

After collecting the yellow gem, a new tunnel opens leading to Area 5.

### Area 5: The Mole King's Domain

Area 5 is the pre-boss area containing the Master Gem Gate. Before the gate, there is a rest area with a save point, health refill, and a friendly mole NPC who offers hints about the boss fight.

The Master Gem Gate requires all four gems placed in the correct sequence (green, red, yellow, blue, as described above). Once the gate opens, you enter the boss arena.

### Area 6: The Mole King Boss Fight

![Mole King boss arena](/images/worlds/detail/mines-boss.svg)

The Mole King is the boss of the Mole Mines. He is a massive mole wearing a crown of crystals and wielding a giant pickaxe. For strategies on every boss in the game, check our [boss fight strategy guide](/blog/boss-fight-strategy/).

The Mole King fight has three distinct phases.

Phase one involves the Mole King slamming his pickaxe into the ground, creating shockwaves that travel outward in straight lines. Jump over the shockwaves and attack him during the recovery animation after each slam. Three attack windows are needed to complete phase one.

Phase two has the Mole King burrowing underground and surfacing at random points in the arena, similar to the digger moles but much larger. When he surfaces, he creates a large area-of-effect tremor that stuns you if you are standing on the ground. To avoid this, stand on one of the raised rock platforms around the edge of the arena. After surfacing, he is dazed for about three seconds. Drop down and attack him during this window.

Phase three combines both previous mechanics. The Mole King stands in the center and alternates between pickaxe slams and burrowing dives. He also summons armored mole minions throughout this phase. Clear the minions when you can, but prioritize dodging the Mole King's attacks and hitting him during his vulnerability windows.

After defeating the Mole King, he drops the Crystal Crown, a key item needed for a late-game overworld mission. He also drops a large cache of coins and the Mole Mines completion badge.

## All Secrets in the Mole Mines

The Mole Mines has 10 secrets to discover. Finding them all unlocks the Miner Bear cosmetic hat. For more hidden items and easter eggs across the entire game, visit our [secret areas and easter eggs guide](/blog/secret-areas-easter-eggs/).

1. **Crystal Cave Sticker** -- Left side tunnel in the Upper Tunnels, behind a group of digger moles.
2. **Hidden Coin Cache** -- Lower level of the Crystal Caverns, accessible by dropping down from the main platform.
3. **Green Gem Alcove** -- Same lower level area as Secret 2, in a hidden alcove behind crystal formations.
4. **Side Track Room** -- During the Mine Cart Express, take the hidden right branch before the second chasm gap.
5. **Sound Puzzle Room** -- In the Deep Tunnels, follow a specific sequence of sound cues (drip-rumble-drip-chime) to find a hidden door.
6. **Behind the Waterfall** -- An underground waterfall in the Deep Tunnels hides a cave passage behind it containing 200 coins.
7. **Crystal Cavern Ceiling** -- Use the crystal light beams as platforms (they become solid after solving the light puzzle) to reach a platform on the ceiling with a sticker.
8. **Mole NPC Secret** -- Talk to the friendly mole NPC in Area 5 three times. On the third conversation, he gives you a map fragment revealing a secret in Area 1 you likely missed.
9. **Boss Arena Pre-fight** -- Before triggering the boss, explore the edges of the arena. A breakable wall on the south side leads to a small room with the final sticker.
10. **Post-Boss Tunnel** -- After defeating the Mole King, his burrowing opens a new tunnel in the arena floor. Drop in to find a bonus room with 500 coins and a rare skin unlock. Check our [skins and hats guide](/blog/unlock-all-skins-hats/) for details.

## Tips for Mastering the Mole Mines

### Mining Cart Survival Tips

Memorize obstacle patterns. Each cart section has a fixed layout, so repeated runs let you learn the sequence. Focus on survival first, then optimize for coin collection on subsequent runs.

When approaching jumps over gaps, keep in mind that the cart has slight forward momentum. Jump a tiny bit earlier than you think you need to. Late jumps are the number one cause of cart section failures.

### Gem Puzzle Tips

Keep track of which gems you have collected. The game does show your gem inventory in the pause menu, but it is easy to forget which puzzles you have already solved. If you are missing a gem and cannot remember which one, the friendly mole NPC in Area 5 gives hints about gem locations.

### Combat Tips in Darkness

In the Deep Tunnels, use the ground pound attack frequently. Its area-of-effect damage can hit mole enemies you cannot see in the darkness. The shockwave from a ground pound briefly illuminates a larger area, acting as a sort of sonar.

### Performance Tips

The Mole Mines has heavy lighting and particle effects, especially in the Crystal Caverns. If you experience frame drops or lag, our [lag fix and optimization guide](/blog/lag-fix-optimization/) covers settings adjustments that can help without sacrificing too much visual quality.
