## Cosmetics in Super Bear Adventure

Super Bear Adventure features an extensive cosmetics system that lets you customize your bear with dozens of skins and hats. While cosmetics are primarily visual, some items provide minor passive bonuses that can help with gameplay. The cosmetic system is one of the game's most engaging features, giving players long-term goals to work toward across all game modes.

Cosmetics fall into two categories: skins (which change your bear's entire appearance or color) and hats (which add headwear and accessories). You can equip one skin and one hat simultaneously, allowing for hundreds of unique combinations. Skins and hats are managed through the Wardrobe menu, accessible from the pause screen or from the wardrobe closet in the overworld hub.

Whether you are a completionist who wants every item or you are looking for a specific cosmetic, this guide covers every skin and hat in the game, how to unlock each one, and which ones are worth prioritizing. If you are just starting the game, our [beginner guide](/blog/how-to-play-beginner-guide/) will help you get oriented before diving into cosmetic hunting.

## All Skins in Super Bear Adventure

### Default Skins

These skins are available from the start or unlocked through basic progression.

**Classic Brown Bear** -- Available from the start. The default bear appearance. A warm brown color with a friendly expression.

**Polar Bear** -- Unlocked by completing the first mission in the overworld hub. Changes your bear to a white color scheme.

**Panda Bear** -- Unlocked by finding 100 total coins across any worlds. Black and white panda pattern.

**Sun Bear** -- Unlocked by completing Turtletown. Golden-brown color with a lighter chest mark.

### World Completion Skins

Each world awards a unique skin upon completion. These are some of the most visually distinctive skins in the game.

**Frost Skin** -- Unlocked by defeating the Frost Giant boss in Snow Valley. Gives your bear an icy blue appearance with frost particles. This skin provides a minor cold resistance passive, reducing damage from ice-based hazards by 10 percent.

**Mummy Wrap** -- Unlocked by defeating the Sand Pharaoh boss in Beemothep Desert. Wraps your bear in tattered bandages with glowing eyes. Purely cosmetic but one of the most popular skins in the community.

**Honey Bear** -- Unlocked by defeating the Queen Bee boss in The Hive. Coats your bear in golden honey with a glossy shine. Provides a minor bonus to honey mechanic interactions in The Hive, making honey walls slightly stickier (easier to hold on to).

**Crystal Bear** -- Unlocked by defeating the Mole King boss in the Mole Mines. Your bear's body becomes semi-transparent with crystal formations. Provides a faint glow in dark areas, which is helpful in the Mole Mines' Deep Tunnels.

**Pixel Bear** -- Unlocked by defeating The Glitch boss in the Arcade World. Transforms your bear into a retro 8-bit pixel art style. One of the most unique visual skins in the game.

**Tiny Bear** -- Unlocked by completing the Giant House world. Adds a visual sparkle effect and makes your bear appear slightly smaller (cosmetic only, hitbox does not change).

For details on each boss encounter, see our [boss fight strategy guide](/blog/boss-fight-strategy/).

### Secret Skins

These skins require special actions or discoveries to unlock.

**Golden Bear** -- Unlocked by collecting all 15 secret stickers. The most prestigious skin in the game, covering your bear in shimmering gold with sparkle particles. See our [sticker locations guide](/blog/all-secret-stickers-locations/) for help finding them all.

**Shadow Bear** -- Unlocked by completing every world without taking any damage in the boss fight. This is an extremely challenging achievement that requires mastery of every boss's attack patterns. The skin turns your bear into a dark silhouette with glowing eyes.

**Rainbow Bear** -- Unlocked by finding all secret areas across every world. Your bear cycles through rainbow colors continuously. Our [secret areas and easter eggs guide](/blog/secret-areas-easter-eggs/) can help you find every hidden area.

**Ghost Bear** -- Unlocked by dying 100 times total (cumulative across all play sessions). A semi-transparent ghost version of your bear with a faint blue glow. This is a consolation skin that you naturally unlock through regular play.

**Astronaut Bear** -- Unlocked by reaching the secret space area in the overworld hub. To access this area, complete all main missions and then interact with the telescope on the hilltop at night (in-game time). A special cutscene plays and you are transported to a small space area with the skin unlock.

### Boss Drop Hats

Each boss drops a unique hat upon defeat. These are among the most coveted hats because they signify that you have beaten challenging content.

**Turtle Shell Hat** -- Dropped by the Turtle King in Turtletown. A miniature turtle shell worn on the head.

**Ice Crown** -- Dropped by the Frost Giant in Snow Valley. A crown made of ice crystals.

**Pharaoh's Headdress** -- Dropped by the Sand Pharaoh in Beemothep Desert. An ornate golden Egyptian headdress.

**Bee Antenna Hat** -- Dropped by the Queen Bee in The Hive. Cute bouncing bee antennae on a headband.

**Cat Ears Hat** -- Dropped by the House Cat in the Giant House. Fluffy cat ears that wiggle when you move.

**Miner Helmet** -- Dropped by the Mole King in the Mole Mines. A classic mining helmet with a working headlamp. This hat has a functional effect in the Mole Mines, providing extra light in the Deep Tunnels.

**Pixel Crown** -- Dropped by The Glitch in the Arcade World. A pixelated crown in 8-bit style.

### Hidden Hats

These hats are found in secret locations throughout the game. Finding them requires exploration and often puzzle-solving.

**Viking Helmet** -- Found in Snow Valley. Hidden inside a frozen ice block near the mountain peak. Ground pound the ice block three times to break it. Check our [Snow Valley guide](/blog/snow-valley-secrets/) for the exact location.

**Flower Crown** -- Found in the Turtletown beach area. Plant three seeds in the garden plots near the turtle village and wait for them to grow (takes about two minutes of real time). The flowers grow into a crown that your bear can pick up.

**Wizard Hat** -- Found in the Beemothep Desert. Complete the Sphinx riddle puzzle to access a hidden chamber. The wizard hat is in a chest inside this chamber. See our [Beemothep Desert guide](/blog/beemothep-desert-puzzle/) for puzzle solutions.

**Propeller Hat** -- Found in the Giant House world. On the toy shelf, interact with the toy helicopter to receive the Propeller Hat. This hat has a subtle effect: it slightly extends your air time when jumping.

**Mushroom Cap** -- Found in the Overworld Hub. Behind the mushroom circle in the forest area, a hidden path leads to a fairy ring where the Mushroom Cap sits on a toadstool.

**Detective Hat** -- Found after completing all [missions](/blog/all-missions-guide/) in the overworld. The mission-giver NPC awards this hat as a final reward.

**Ninja Headband** -- Found in The Hive. Jump into the royal jelly column in the Royal Jelly Chamber to be transported to a hidden room. The Ninja Headband is on a shelf in this room. Full details in our [Hive walkthrough](/blog/the-hive-walkthrough/).

**Hard Hat** -- Found in the Mole Mines post-boss tunnel. After defeating the Mole King, explore the tunnel his burrowing opened in the arena floor. The Hard Hat is in the bonus room. See our [Mole Mines walkthrough](/blog/mole-mines-walkthrough/) for the full path.

### Event and Special Hats

These hats are available during special events or through specific conditions.

**Santa Hat** -- Available during the December holiday event. Log in during the event period to receive it automatically.

**Pumpkin Head** -- Available during the October Halloween event. Complete the special Halloween mission to earn it.

**Birthday Hat** -- Awarded on the anniversary of your first time playing the game. The game tracks the date of your first play session.

**Multiplayer Crown** -- Earned by winning 10 multiplayer matches. See our [multiplayer guide](/blog/multiplayer-guide/) for tips on winning matches.

## Cosmetics in Multiplayer

Your equipped cosmetics appear in [multiplayer mode](/blog/multiplayer-guide/), and certain cosmetics serve as status symbols. Players wearing the Golden Bear skin or Shadow Bear skin are instantly recognized as experienced players. The Multiplayer Crown hat is exclusive to successful multiplayer competitors.

Cosmetics do not provide any gameplay advantages in multiplayer. The minor passive effects from skins and hats are disabled in competitive multiplayer to ensure fair play. They remain active in cooperative multiplayer modes.
