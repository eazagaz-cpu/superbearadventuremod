Baaren is the protagonist of Super Bear Adventure. Understanding every ability in his moveset — both in standard gameplay and in Super Bear Fighters — is the difference between struggling and mastering the game.

## Baaren's Core Movement Abilities

### 1. Double Jump

The double jump is Baaren's most fundamental ability and is available from the very beginning of the game.

**How to use:** Press Jump once to leave the ground. While airborne, press Jump again to perform the second jump.

**Applications:**
- Reaching platforms that a single jump cannot reach
- Extending air time for precise landings
- Chaining into the Glide ability at the peak of the second jump
- Avoiding enemy attacks mid-air

**Tips:**
- Wait until near the peak of the first jump before pressing Jump again — this maximizes overall height
- The second jump refreshes when you land on any solid surface, including enemy heads
- Wall-jumping (if the wall allows it) also refreshes the double jump

**In boss fights:** The double jump is essential for staying airborne to avoid ground-based boss attacks. Practice the timing for each boss's attack windows.

### 2. Pounce Attack

The pounce is Baaren's main aerial combat move — a targeted downward slam attack performed in the air.

**How to use:** While airborne, press Attack (not jump) with Baaren angled downward toward an enemy.

**Applications:**
- Aerial enemies below Baaren's position
- Starting combos on grounded enemies
- Dealing high damage to large enemies and bosses

**Tips:**
- The pounce has a larger horizontal hitbox than it appears — you do not need to be directly above the target
- Pounce → land → immediately attack is the fastest combo starter in the game
- On large enemies, the pounce staggers them, giving you a window for follow-up attacks

**In boss fights:** Almost every boss has a phase where they are vulnerable from above. The pounce is the optimal damage dealer during these windows. See the [boss fight guide](/blog/boss-fight-strategy/) for specific applications.

### 3. Roll Dodge

The roll is a short-range quick dodge that makes Baaren invincible for the roll duration.

**How to use:** Double-tap the movement direction rapidly (left-left or right-right). Alternatively, press the dedicated dodge button if the control scheme has one.

**Applications:**
- Avoiding incoming enemy attacks
- Getting past narrow enemy formations
- Repositioning faster than walking allows
- Canceling into attacks from the roll (roll → attack timing)

**Tips:**
- The roll has a brief invincibility window at the start — this is the key to dodging attacks that would otherwise be unavoidable
- Rolling into an enemy does not deal damage — it passes through them
- The roll has a short recovery animation at the end; do not spam it back-to-back

### 4. Slide

The slide allows Baaren to move quickly at ground level under low obstacles.

**How to use:** While running, press and hold Crouch or Down. Baaren automatically enters a slide when running speed is sufficient.

**Applications:**
- Passing under obstacles that jumping would not clear
- Several missions in the Desert and Giant House require the slide
- Speedrunning technique — the slide maintains more momentum than walking turns

**Tips:**
- You cannot attack during a slide but you can cancel it by releasing crouch
- Some collectibles and stickers are hidden in spaces only accessible via slide
- The slide can be used on slopes for additional speed (slope slide technique)

### 5. Glide

The glide extends Baaren's air time significantly after jumping, allowing larger horizontal distances.

**How to use:** At the peak of a jump (or double jump), hold Jump to enter glide mode. Baaren extends his cape/arms and falls slowly.

**Applications:**
- Crossing large gaps
- Reaching distant platforms
- Soft-landing without fall damage
- Reconnaissance — gliding gives time to see what is below before committing to a landing

**Tips:**
- The glide works best from the peak of a double jump (maximum starting height)
- You cannot attack during a glide; release the hold to drop normally and attack
- In multiplayer, coordinating glides with teammates allows group gap-crossing

## Combat Moves

### Basic Attack

The basic jab is a quick melee strike with short range and low damage. It is fastest to execute and best for fighting small, fast enemies.

**Best use:** Clearing standard enemies quickly. Not ideal for boss fights where high damage per swing matters.

### Charged Strike

Hold the attack button to charge, then release for a powerful strike.

**Charge time:** ~1 second to full charge.

**Damage:** 3–4× the basic jab.

**Best use:** Bosses and large enemies during vulnerable windows. The wind-up makes it risky against fast enemies.

**Tips:**
- You can move slowly while charging
- Release at the perfect moment during an enemy's stagger for maximum effect
- In Super Bear Fighters PvP, the Charged Strike is your main damage tool

## Baaren in Super Bear Fighters

In the [Super Bear Fighters mini-game](/blog/super-bear-adventure-super-bear-fighters/), Baaren has access to all the same moves but in a 2D fighting context:

- Double jump for platform navigation
- Pounce for aerial combat
- Charged Strike for high-damage punishes
- Roll for dodge mechanics

Each skin also adds a unique special move. The standard Baaren (default skin) has the **Spin Attack** — a 360-degree AoE spin that hits both sides simultaneously.

## Ability Progression

Baaren's abilities are available from the game's start. There is no traditional RPG-style level-up system. The progression is in the player's skill with each ability rather than unlocking new ones.

The game's worlds introduce new ability requirements progressively:

- Turtletown: Double jump, basic combat
- Snow Valley: Slide (ice sections), glide (cliff gaps)
- Desert: All abilities in combination (pyramid platforming)
- Giant House: Precise double jump (tall furniture)
- The Hive: Roll dodge (tight corridors, bee projectiles)
- Mole Mines: All abilities at peak difficulty

By Mole Mines, players who have mastered all abilities find the world significantly more manageable than those who have only learned the basics.
