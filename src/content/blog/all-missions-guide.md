## How Missions Work in Super Bear Adventure

Missions are the backbone of Super Bear Adventure. They drive your progression through each world, unlock new areas, and provide the coins and rewards you need to access everything the game offers. Understanding how the mission system works before diving into individual objectives saves time and prevents backtracking.

Every world in the game contains a set of main missions and optional side objectives. Main missions must be completed to advance the story and unlock access to the next world. Side missions are optional but reward you with coins, cosmetics, stickers, and other valuable items.

Missions are given by NPCs scattered throughout each world. Look for characters with markers above their heads. Talk to them to receive the objective, then complete the task and return to the same NPC for your reward. Some missions chain together, meaning completing one unlocks the next in a sequence.

Your mission log tracks active objectives and shows brief descriptions of what you need to do. Check it whenever you feel lost. If you are brand new to the game, our [beginner's guide](/blog/how-to-play-beginner-guide/) covers the fundamentals of controls and navigation before you start tackling missions.


## Mission Types Explained

Super Bear Adventure uses several mission archetypes that repeat across worlds with variations.

- **Fetch Missions:** Find and return specific items hidden in side paths, breakable objects, or guarded areas.
- **Defeat Missions:** Clear out a group of enemies from a specific area. Enemy count and types vary by world.
- **Escort Missions:** Guide an NPC safely from one point to another while fighting off threats.
- **Exploration Missions:** Reach a specific location through platforming or puzzle-solving.
- **Puzzle Missions:** Solve environmental puzzles by moving blocks, activating switches, or manipulating objects.
- **Boss Missions:** The final main mission in each world, pitting you against a major boss. Our [boss fight strategy guide](/blog/boss-fight-strategy/) has detailed tactics.
- **Collection Missions:** Side objectives to gather collectibles like coins, stickers, or world-specific items.


## Turtletown Missions

Turtletown is the first world and serves as your introduction to the game's mission structure. Difficulty is low, and the missions teach core mechanics. For a complete area walkthrough, see our [Turtletown walkthrough](/blog/turtletown-walkthrough/).

### Main Missions

1. **Welcome to Turtletown** (Exploration) - Follow the path to the hilltop lookout. Teaches movement and camera controls. Reward: 50 coins.
2. **Turtle Trouble** (Defeat) - Defeat 10 turtles in the eastern meadow. Sidestep their charges and counter-attack. Reward: 75 coins.
3. **The Lost Shell** (Fetch) - Find the golden shell in the forest cave behind the waterfall east of the village. Double jump required. Reward: 100 coins + forest access.
4. **Bridge Builder** (Fetch) - Collect 5 wooden planks scattered around the village area to repair the northern bridge. Reward: 100 coins + northern access.
5. **The Village Elder's Request** (Escort) - Escort the elder to the hilltop shrine. Clear enemies ahead of him. Reward: 150 coins.
6. **King Turtle Showdown** (Boss) - Defeat King Turtle in three phases: straight charges, shell spins, and summoning minions. Attack during recovery windows. Reward: 200 coins + Snow Valley key.

### Side Missions

- **Coin Collector:** Gather 200 coins across Turtletown. Reward: Turtle Hat cosmetic.
- **Sticker Hunt:** Find all 8 stickers. Reward: 150 coins. See our [all secret stickers locations guide](/blog/all-secret-stickers-locations/).
- **The Fisherman's Favor:** Bring 3 fish to the fisherman. Reward: 75 coins + health upgrade.
- **Crate Smasher:** Destroy 50 crates. Reward: 100 coins.


## Snow Valley Missions

Snow Valley introduces environmental hazards, ice physics, and more complex platforming. For hidden secrets and collectible locations, visit our [Snow Valley secrets guide](/blog/snow-valley-secrets/).

### Main Missions

1. **Cold Welcome** (Exploration) - Reach the village through the mountain pass. Introduces ice surfaces and sliding control. Reward: 75 coins.
2. **Frozen Supplies** (Fetch) - Recover 6 supply crates from the frozen lake area. The slippery surface makes this tricky. Reward: 100 coins.
3. **Wolf Pack Elimination** (Defeat) - Defeat 15 ice wolves in the pine forest. They are faster and hit harder than turtles. Kite them apart. Reward: 125 coins.
4. **The Frozen Key** (Puzzle) - Solve sliding block puzzles in the ice cavern to obtain the Frozen Key. Exit and re-enter to reset blocks if stuck. Reward: 150 coins + upper mountain access.
5. **Frost Bear's Lair** (Boss) - Defeat Frost Bear through three phases: snowball throws, bowling charge, and freezing breath. Reward: 250 coins + Beemothep Desert key.

### Side Missions

- **Snowman Builder:** Find 5 snowman parts. Reward: Snowman Hat cosmetic.
- **Ice Fishing Challenge:** Catch 5 fish through ice holes within a time limit. Reward: 100 coins.
- **Lost Mountaineer:** Find and escort the mountaineer to the village. Reward: 125 coins.
- **Snow Valley Stickers:** Find all 8 stickers. Reward: 150 coins.

3. **Pyramid Entrance** (Puzzle) - Solve three sand dial puzzles matching sun, moon, and star symbols to open the pyramid gate. Reward: 100 coins + pyramid access.
4. **Tomb Raider** (Puzzle) - Navigate the pyramid maze with traps and sliding block puzzles to retrieve the Golden Scarab. Reward: 200 coins.
5. **The Sandstorm** (Exploration) - Cross the sandstorm by following stone pillars. Straying off-path deals damage. Reward: 100 coins.
6. **Scorpion King Battle** (Boss) - Defeat the Scorpion King. Attack when his stinger gets stuck after tail slams. Three phases. Reward: 300 coins + Giant House key.

### Side Missions

- **Water Bearer:** Deliver 4 water jugs to NPCs. Reward: 100 coins.
- **Relic Hunter:** Find 6 ancient relics. Reward: Pharaoh Hat cosmetic.
- **Cactus Destroyer:** Smash 30 cacti. Reward: 75 coins.
- **Desert Stickers:** Find all 10 stickers. Reward: 200 coins.


## Giant House Missions

6. **The Dusty Library** (Puzzle) - Arrange colored books in the correct order using clues from living room paintings. Reward: 150 coins.
7. **Cat Catastrophe** (Boss) - Defeat the House Cat across four phases. Hide behind furniture during pounces, strike during stun windows. Reward: 350 coins + The Hive key.

- **Crumb Collector:** Collect 100 crumbs from the kitchen. Reward: 100 coins.

3. **Worker Bee Revolt** (Defeat) - Defeat 20 Worker Bees using jump attacks. Soldier Bees appear midway with more health. Reward: 150 coins.
4. **Royal Jelly Heist** (Stealth) - Steal 3 Royal Jelly containers by avoiding guard vision cones. Reward: 200 coins + Queen's chamber access.
5. **Pollen Transport** (Escort) - Guide the pollen cart through three chambers by activating track switches. Reward: 150 coins.
6. **Queen Bee Confrontation** (Boss) - Aerial boss fight using bounce pads to reach her. Three phases with increasing minion waves. Reward: 400 coins + Mole Mines key.

### Side Missions

- **Honey Taster:** Sample honey from 5 chambers. Reward: Bee Antenna Hat cosmetic.
- **Larva Rescue:** Return 4 lost larvae to the nursery. Reward: 150 coins.
- **Hive Stickers:** Find all 8 stickers. Reward: 175 coins.

## Best Order to Complete Missions

While the game gives you some freedom, following the intended progression path provides the smoothest experience in terms of difficulty scaling and story flow.

**Recommended progression:**

1. Complete all Turtletown main missions first. These teach mechanics you need everywhere else.
2. Do Turtletown side missions to build your coin reserve before moving on.
3. Progress through Snow Valley. The difficulty step up is manageable with Turtletown experience.
4. Tackle Beemothep Desert. Puzzle skills learned here help in Giant House.
5. Clear Giant House. The platforming challenges prepare you for The Hive.
6. Take on The Hive. Aerial combat training here pays off against the Mole King.
7. Finish with Mole Mines. This is the most demanding world and benefits from all prior experience.

**Within each world,** complete main missions in order since they often gate access to subsequent areas. Do side missions between main missions when you want a break from the critical path, or save them for a cleanup pass after finishing the world's boss.

## Tips for Difficult Missions

Some missions are significantly harder than others. Here are strategies for the most common sticking points.

**Timed missions (Dynamite Run, etc.):** Memorize the route first by running it without the objective. Once you know every turn and obstacle, the time pressure becomes manageable.

**Escort missions:** Clear enemies ahead of the NPC or cart rather than reacting to attacks. Proactive clearing prevents damage to what you are protecting.

**Stealth sections (Royal Jelly Heist):** Be patient. Wait for full guard rotation cycles before moving. Rushing leads to detection and heavy damage.

**Boss fights:** Every boss has a pattern. If you are dying repeatedly, spend an attempt just watching and dodging without attacking. Learn the full pattern, then use your next attempt to capitalize on vulnerability windows. The [boss fight strategy guide](/blog/boss-fight-strategy/) provides all patterns.

**Platforming challenges (Bookshelf, Hive climbing):** Adjust your camera before every jump. A bad camera angle is the most common cause of missed platforms. Take your time, there is no rush penalty on platforming missions.

**Dark areas (Mole Mines):** Always carry a torch. Missing a torch pickup does not mean one is not available. Check wall brackets you may have passed. Some are hidden around corners.


## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I replay completed missions?

Yes. You can replay any completed mission by talking to the original quest giver. Replayed missions reward reduced coins but are useful for practicing boss fights or finding missed collectibles.

### Do I have to complete side missions to progress?

No. Only main missions are required to unlock the next world. Side missions are entirely optional but provide valuable coins and unique cosmetic rewards.

### What happens if I fail a mission?

You respawn at the last checkpoint within the mission area. For timed missions, the timer resets. For escort missions, the NPC or cart returns to its starting position. You lose a life but keep any coins collected during the attempt.

### Are there any hidden missions not listed in the mission log?

Yes. Some NPCs offer missions only after you discover them in hidden locations. These are not tracked in the mission log until accepted. Exploring off the main paths frequently reveals these hidden quest givers. Our [secret areas and easter eggs guide](/blog/secret-areas-easter-eggs/) documents their locations.

### Can I complete missions in multiplayer?

Most missions can be completed in co-op multiplayer. Boss missions and timed missions are generally more manageable with a partner. See the [multiplayer guide](/blog/multiplayer-guide/) for co-op mission tips.

### Which mission gives the most coins?

The Mole King boss fight rewards 500 coins, making it the single highest-paying mission. However, factoring in difficulty and time, the Crystal Cavern fetch mission (175 coins in about 5 minutes) offers one of the best coin-per-minute rates.

### Do mission rewards change in the mod version?

The mod version may adjust coin rewards or add bonus multipliers depending on the build. Check our [version changelogs](/blog/version-changelogs/) for mod-specific changes, and our [old versions archive](/blog/super-bear-adventure-mod-apk-old-versions/) if you need a specific build.
